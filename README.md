Messi no longer sprints the length of the pitch, but his genius in tight pockets between the lines is undimmed. Whoever picks him up — likely a rotating job between Rice dropping and Bellingham tracking — must resist being drawn out of shape.

- **The danger:** Messi drifts inside off the right, collecting between England's lines to thread De Paul, Álvarez and Lautaro through the gaps.
- **England's answer:** Rice screens the space, while Stones steps out to press early before Messi turns.
- **The risk:** Over-commit, and Messi's set-piece delivery punishes any lapse in the box.

## Battle 3 — Saka vs Tagliafico

Out wide, **Bukayo Saka** against left-back **Nicolás Tagliafico** is a match-up brimming with intrigue. Saka's direct running and end product on the right make him England's most reliable creative outlet, while Tagliafico is a seasoned, tenacious defender who rarely gives an inch.

- **Saka's weapon:** cutting inside onto his left foot or overlapping to the byline with Alexander-Arnold's support.
- **Tagliafico's plan:** stay tight, force Saka backward, and rely on De Paul tucking in to double up.
- **The swing:** if Saka gets isolated in one-vs-one situations, he can win England the game on his own.

This <a href="#">Saka vs Tagliafico</a> flank duel is where England's structured attack meets Argentina's disciplined defending — a genuine game-within-the-game.
